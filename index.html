Projects

Things I built because I wanted them to exist. The web tools are free and run client-side.

Ascent Strava

A personal Strava analytics dashboard: stats, charts, a ride heatmap, segment PRs, gear tracking, trophies, and shareable 9:16 story cards for your rides. Connects to your own Strava account with your permission — your tokens and activities stay in your browser.

Open Ascent Source

Bike Fit Analyzer Cycling

Upload a side-on video of yourself pedalling and get automatic joint-angle analysis (knee, hip, back) graded against bike-fitting research, to help you fine-tune saddle height and reach. The video is analyzed on your device and never uploaded. It is not a professional bike fit and not medical advice.

Open Bike Fit Source

SPMB Bali 2026 Data

An analysis of the academic-track entry scores at all 88 public high schools across Bali's nine districts, ranked into tiers so a student can see where they realistically stand. It also makes the regional gap hard to look away from — the distance between Denpasar's schools and those on the outer islands is not small.

Open SPMB Bali Source

Terrafarms Agritech

An AI and IoT platform for Indonesian farmers: soil analysis, crop recommendations, plant disease detection, real-time nutrition monitoring, and a route to market for seed and fertiliser supply. Built with a team, out of the conviction that smallholders deserve the same decision-making tools that large operations already have.

Open Terrafarms

Aksara Bali Culture

Converts Latin text into Balinese script in real time, with Sanskrit support and V=W equivalency — an educational tool for reading, writing and preserving Aksara Bali.

Open Aksara Bali Source

About

The short version.

I am Doni Wirawan. I am the person behind the web side of Studio Five Group: the planning dashboards, delivery trackers and website builder its wedding clients use, and the sites for each company in the group — Weddings, Spaces, Corp, Pixels and Printhouse — along with the marine fuel side: Straits Bunkering in Singapore and Dubai, Eng Hua and Sirius Oil. I build them, and I keep them running.

Before that I graduated from Bangkit, Google's academy in Indonesia, where my team and I built Terrafarms — an AI and IoT platform that gives farmers soil analysis, crop recommendations and disease detection. I also founded the Google Developer Student Club at INSTIKI and led it as its first chapter lead, which is still the thing I am proudest of: it outlived my involvement, which is the only real test of whether you built something or just ran it.

The rest of the time I build small free tools for the things I care about: my own riding data with Ascent, my own bike fit with Bike Fit Analyzer, and the Balinese script I grew up around with Aksara Bali. They all run in the browser, they are all free, and none of them upload what you put into them. That is a deliberate choice, not a limitation — I would rather ship something small that respects you than something big that does not.

Cycling

The reason two of these apps exist.

I ride road and gravel around Bali — early mornings, long climbs, and the odd sprint for a segment I have no business chasing. It is where I do my best thinking, and it is also where most of my ideas for tools come from.

Ascent started because I wanted to see my own Strava data the way I actually think about it. The Bike Fit Analyzer started because I kept fiddling with my saddle height and wanted numbers instead of guesses. If you ride too, both of them are free — and I would genuinely like to know what they get wrong.
